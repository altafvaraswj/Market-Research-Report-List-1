<p><p>The Reverse Osmosis (RO) membrane market comprises two primary types: composite membranes and asymmetric membranes. Composite membranes consist of thin, selective layers typically made from polymer materials, providing high efficiency and salt rejection while maintaining permeability. Asymmetric membranes have a varying structure, featuring a dense top layer supported by a porous substrate, allowing for effective filtration with lower operational pressure. Both types play crucial roles in water purification, desalination, and industrial processes, catering to diverse application needs.</p></p>
