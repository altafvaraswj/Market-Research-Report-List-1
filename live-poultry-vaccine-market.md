<p><p>The live poultry vaccine market consists primarily of two types: injectable vaccines and spray vaccines. Injectable vaccines are administered through syringes, providing targeted immunity and effective disease prevention in poultry. They are often preferred for their precision and rapid immune response. In contrast, spray vaccines are aerosolized and applied directly to flocks in enclosed environments, facilitating mass vaccination without individual handling. This method is efficient for large populations and promotes uniform exposure, enhancing overall flock health and biosecurity.</p></p>
