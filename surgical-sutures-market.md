<p><p>The surgical sutures market is divided into two main types: absorbable and non-absorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are designed to break down and dissolve in the body over time, eliminating the need for removal, making them ideal for internal tissues and wounds. Non-absorbable sutures, on the other hand, are intended for permanent use, often employed in skin or other external applications, requiring surgical removal. Both types play crucial roles in various surgical procedures, enhancing wound healing and patient recovery.</p></p>
