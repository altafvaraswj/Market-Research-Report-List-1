<p><p>The ozone detector market primarily includes ultraviolet photometers and electrochemical sensors. Ultraviolet photometers measure ozone concentrations based on light absorption at specific wavelengths, offering high accuracy and real-time monitoring. In contrast, electrochemical sensors operate through chemical reactions that produce a measurable electrical current proportional to ozone levels. Both types serve diverse applications, including air quality monitoring, industrial emissions control, and environmental research, providing critical data for health and regulatory compliance. Their effectiveness supports various sectors committed to maintaining safe ozone levels.</p></p>
