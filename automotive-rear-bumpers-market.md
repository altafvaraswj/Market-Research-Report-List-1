Plastic rear bumpers, typically made from materials like polypropylene, are lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and cost-effective, making them popular for fuel efficiency and design flexibility. Conversely, metal rear bumpers, often constructed from steel or aluminum, provide superior durability and impact resistance but can be heavier and more expensive.
